# "The Necklace" short film script
**Original work: Maupassant**
**Adaptation: Short Film Version**
**Duration: About 8-10 minutes**

---

## Cast

| Role | Age | Description |
|------|------|------|
| Mathilde Loisel | 25 years old/35 years old | Beautiful, from a family of small clerks, yearning for a high life |
| Loisel | 30/40 years old | Junior staff member of the Ministry of Education, frugal and simple, deeply in love with his wife |
| Mrs. Forestier | 25 years old/45 years old | Mathilde’s female classmate, from a wealthy family |

---

## scene 1: Loisel’s Residence·Night

**INT. Dim little room**

Mathilde stood in front of a mottled mirror. At the age of twenty-five, she had a beautiful face, but her eyes were full of melancholy.

She touched her simple dress and sighed.

The camera panned the room: old furniture, rough walls, tablecloths that were only changed every three days.

Loisel pushed open the door and entered with a large envelope in his hand and a rare pride on his face.

<center>Loisel</center>
>Look, there's something here just for you.

Mathilde took the envelope and opened it. An invitation card with gold stamping.

<center>Close-up of invitation</center>
> **Minister of Education Jorge Lambono and his wife**
> **Honoured to invite Mr. and Mrs. Loisel**
> **Attend the party on Monday, January 18th at the headquarters building**

Mathilde stared at the invitation and burst into tears.

<center>Loser (panic)</center>
> How are you doing? How much effort did it take me to get this invitation...

<center>Matilde (choked)</center>
> What did you ask me to wear there?

Loisel was stunned. He saw his wife's tears and finally understood.

<center>Loisel (hesitate)</center>
> How much does it cost... for a decent set of clothes?

<center>Mathilde (wiping tears, calculating)</center>
>With four hundred gold francs... it can be done.

Loisel's face turned slightly blue - he had saved the money to buy a shotgun.

But he still said:

<center>Loisel</center>
>That's it. I'll give you four hundred gold francs.

---

## scene 2: Mrs. Forai Shijie’s House·Day

**INT. LUXURY LIVING ROOM**

Madame Forestier opened a large mirrored wardrobe.

Inside are various jewelry: pearl necklaces, gem crosses, diamond bracelets...

Mathilde's eyes lit up. She tried them on one by one and looked at them in front of the mirror, never satisfied.

<center>Matilde</center>
> Do you have anything else?

<center>Mrs. Forresty</center>
> There are some, you can find them yourself.

Mathilde's eyes fell on a small black satin box.

She opened the box—

**Close-up: A string of diamond necklaces shines under lighting**

Mathilde's heartbeat quickened. With trembling hands, she put the necklace around her neck and spent a long time lost in thought in front of the mirror.

She looked beautiful and noble in the mirror, like another person.

<center>Matilde (hesitantly)</center>
> Can you lend me this? I only borrow this one.

<center>Mrs. Forresty</center>
> Of course.

Mathilde jumped up to Setup, hugged her friend's neck and kissed her passionately.

---

## The third session: Ministry of Education Party·Night

**INT. Magnificent Banquet Hall**

Mathilde enters - she is more beautiful, fashionable and charming than all the female guests.

The male guests stared at her in fascination, asking for her name and vying to dance with her.

The minister noticed her.

Mathilde danced with an intoxicating attitude, immersed in joy. She was satisfied with the triumph of her appearance, satisfied with the flattery and admiration.

**Close-up: Diamond necklace sparkles in the light**

A happy auspicious cloud surrounded her, and she didn't think about anything anymore.

---

## scene 4: Return·Late Night

**EXT./INT. Street/Carriage**

Four o'clock in the morning. Mathilde and Loisel left the venue.

Loisel put a simple cloak on his wife - the poverty of this dress was not commensurate with the luxury of the ball.

Mathilde felt this, and she hurried down the steps, trying to escape.

The two searched for a taxi in the cold wind and finally found a dilapidated carriage.

**Close-up: Mathilde subconsciously touched her neck**

The necklace is still there.

---

## scene 5: Loisel’s Residence·Early Morning

**INT. BEDROOM**

The two returned home exhausted.

Mathilde stood in front of the mirror, wanting to look at her glorious self again.

She took off her cloak——

**Close-up: Mathilde in the mirror, her neck empty**

<center>Mathilde (makes a wild scream)</center>
> I've... I've... I can't find Mrs. Forrest's necklace now!

Loisel stood up at Setup in confusion.

<center>Loisel</center>
>What! ……how! …

The two searched frantically: the pleats of the skirt, the pleats of the cloak, the pockets...

Can't be found anywhere.

---

## scene 6: Looking for Daytime

**EXT. Streets of Paris/Montmartre**

Loisel searched along the route he had taken on foot.

He went to the police headquarters and various newspapers to offer rewards, and went to the taxi company to inquire.

Mathilde sat at home, dressed in party clothes, motionless, thinking of nothing.

One day passed.

Loisel came home in the evening, his face thin and gray.

<center>Loisel</center>
> Found nothing. You should write to your friend and say that the hook has been broken and is being repaired.

Mathilde wrote the letter under his dictation.

---

## scene 7: Jewelry Store·Day

**INT. Every jewelry store**

The couple visited one store after another holding the box containing the necklace.

They relied on their memories to find an identical necklace.

Finally, in a small shop on Forbidden City Street——

**Close-up: A diamond necklace**

<center>jeweler</center>
> Forty thousand gold francs. I can give you thirty-six thousand.

The two looked at each other.

---

## scene 8: Ten Years Montage

**Quick cuts, soundtrack goes from upbeat Turning Point to heavy**

- Loisel signed IOUs one after another
- Mathilde fires her maid
- The two move into the attic under the roof
- Mathilde washes the cups and dishes, her rose-colored fingers become rough
- She went to vegetable shops and grocery stores with her basket in her arm to negotiate prices and was scolded.
- Loisel transcribed accounts and copied books late at night
- Collect IOUs every month and create new ones
- Mathilde's hands become red and chapped.
- She has wrinkles on her face
- Her hair is starting to turn gray.
- Her voice becomes rough.

**Subtitle: Ten Years Later**

<center>Loisel (voice-over)</center>
>We...paid it off.

Mathilde is sitting in front of the window. She is already thirty-five years old, but she looks fifty years old.

She thought about that dance ten years ago. She was so beautiful and happy at that time.

<center>Mathilde (monologue)</center>
> If I hadn’t lost that piece of jewelry... what kind of state would I have reached?

---

## scene 9: Champs Elysées Day

**EXT. Sunny Street**

Mathilde went to get ready for the week's work, and suddenly she saw a woman walking with her child.

That was Mrs. Forestier--always young, beautiful, and alluring.

Mathilde was very excited.

Do you want to talk? She had paid off her debt and could tell her once and for all.

She came closer.

<center>Matilde</center>
> Good morning, Johanne.

Madame Forestier did not recognize her.

<center>Mrs. Forrest (confused)</center>
>But...this lady! ...I don't know...perhaps you are mistaken.

<center>Matilde</center>
>Nothing wrong. My name is Mathilde Loisel.

<center>Mrs. Forrest (screaming)</center>
> Oh! ...Poor Mathilde, you have really changed! …

<center>Matilde</center>
> Yes, I have lived through many very difficult days...all the sufferings are because of you!

<center>Mrs. Forresty</center>
>For me...what's going on?

<center>Matilde</center>
> Once upon a time, didn’t you lend me a diamond necklace for a party? …I lost that bunch of stuff.

<center>Mrs. Forrest (puzzled)</center>
> If not, you would have returned it to me long ago.

<center>Matilde</center>
> What I gave you back was another identical string. It has taken us ten years now to pay the price. Now that the debt has been paid off, I am completely satisfied.

Mathilde smiled with an air of conceited and innocent joy.

Madame Forestier stopped and took her hands.

<center>Mrs. Forresty</center>
> Alas. Poor Mathilde...

**Close-up: Mrs. Forresty's lips are trembling**

<center>Mrs. Forresty</center>
> But my string is fake...

**silence**

<center>Mrs. Forresty</center>
> ... worth five hundred gold francs at most!

---

## scene 10: Ending

**frame freezes on Mathilde’s face**

Her expression slowly solidified from conceited and innocent to an indescribable blankness.

**The camera slowly zooms in on her eyes**

What was inside was slowly extinguished.

**Cut black**

**End credits, fade in:**

> *Life is so weird and so ever-changing. *
> *None can harm you or save you, just a little thing. *

> —— Maupassant's "The Necklace"

**Black screen, fade out**

**【End of the play】**
