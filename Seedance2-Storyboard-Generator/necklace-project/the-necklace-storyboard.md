## Appendix: Core Visual Motif

### 1. Mirror (used throughout the film)
- Opening: A mottled mirror reflects desire
- Middle section: The mirror in Foreskin’s home reflects vanity.
- Climax: When you discover that you are missing, the mirror reflects your empty neck
- Ending: The "mirror" reflected in Mathilde's eyes (mirror of the heart)

### 2. Necklace
- First appearance: sparkling, seductive
- At the dance: eye-catching, symbolizing glory
- After loss: absence, heavy presence
- Ten years later: Shackles in memory

### 3. Hands (a sign of aging)
- Mathilde initially: rosy, tender
- Ten years later: roughness, dryness, and redness
- Comparison with Forrest's hand: a cruel gap

### 4. lighting changes
- Opening: Dim (the depression of a small staff family)
- Borrow necklace: bright (vain light)
- Dance: Brilliant (peak of life)
- Looking for: Cold Blue (Despair)
- Ten years: pale (heaviness of labor)
- Ending: dazzling sunshine (the cruelty of the truth)
