# The Battle of Yashan - Script

## Story Overview

In the second year of Xiangxing in the Southern Song Dynasty (1279), the Song army launched a final decisive battle with the Yuan army in Yashan (now Jiangmen, Guangdong). Lu Xiufu carried the young emperor Zhao Bing on his back and threw himself into the sea to die for his country. Hundreds of thousands of soldiers and civilians also crossed the sea one after another. The Southern Song Dynasty came to a tragic end. This was one of the most brutal naval battles in Chinese history and a landmark event in dynasty change.

## Style positioning

**Visual style**: Epic traditional Chinese ink style, integrating a strong sense of history and tragedy. Refer to the color system of "Han Xizai's Night Banquet" and the composition aesthetics of "Thousand Miles of Rivers and Mountains".

**Target duration**: 120-150 seconds, divided into 8-10 episodes

**Emotional tone**: tragic, solemn, epic

---

## Four-act structure (starting, transitioning, and ending)

### Act 1: Starting - Mountains and Rivers Shattered (0-30 seconds)

**scene setting**: In the late Southern Song Dynasty, thousands of warships gathered on the cliffs and the sea

**Content**:
- In the early spring of the second year of Xiangxing (1279), the sea surface of Yashan was covered with clouds
- Thousands of warships of the Southern Song Dynasty navy lined up in a majestic array
- The young emperor Zhao Bing (7 years old) boarded the imperial boat under the escort of Prime Minister Lu Xiufu
- Lu Xiufu told the young emperor that the current situation was critical and that most of the Song Dynasty had been lost.
- On the horizon in the distance, the dark shadows of Yuan army warships gradually approach
- Howling sea breeze, hunting flags, the depressing atmosphere before the war

**atmosphere**: Depressing, tragic, stormy and imminent

---

### Act 2: Cheng - The eve of the decisive battle (30-60 seconds)

**Content**:
- Zhang Hongfan, the naval commander of the Yuan Army, laid out an iron chain and boat formation
- Zhang Shijie, the commander-in-chief of the Song Army, ordered the palaces on the shore to be burned down in a fight to the death.
- The officers and soldiers of the Song Army were ready to go on the deck, with no fear on their faces
- Lu Xiufu arranged the dragon robe for the young emperor on the royal boat and said softly: "Your Majesty, I will protect you to the death."
- The war drums of the Yuan army shook the sky, and the warships came over the city like black clouds.
- The war drums of the Song Army responded, and the two armies faced each other with arrows on the strings.

**Drama Conflict**: The showdown between the last power of the Song Dynasty and the Yuan army

---

### Act 3: Turn - bloody battle (60-100 seconds)

**Content**:
- The Yuan army launched a general attack, with rockets and fireballs falling like raindrops
- The Song army's warship caught fire and the sea turned into a sea of flames
- The soldiers fought bravely but were outnumbered.
- Zhang Shijie's handsome boat was surrounded, but he still fought hard to command it
- Seeing that the situation was over, Lu Xiufu walked towards the Young Emperor with a solemn expression.
- He knelt in front of the young emperor, with tears streaming down his face: "Your Majesty, the state affairs are at this point, and I can only die to repay the country's kindness."
- The young emperor nodded, with perseverance beyond his years in his eyes.

**Climax**: The ultimate moment of national destruction

---

### Act 4: Together - Heroic sacrifice for the country (100-135 seconds)

**Content**:
- Lu Xiufu tied the imperial seal around his waist and carried the young emperor to the side of the ship.
- He faced the south (direction of Lin'an), knelt down and called out three times: "Your Majesty, Lu Xiufu, bid farewell to your Majesty."
- Leaping forward, the monarch and his subjects plunged into the rolling waves.
- Huge waves splashed on the sea surface, then calmed down
- An even more shocking scene: Tens of thousands of people from the harem, officials, soldiers, and civilians threw themselves into the sea one after another.
- There are countless corpses floating on the sea, and the water is dyed red.
- The setting sun is like blood, reflecting this tragic sea area
- The Southern Song Dynasty came to an end here

**Theme sublimation**: I would rather die than be ruined; the national integrity of people who would rather die than surrender.

## Core information

**Theme**: National integrity that would rather die than surrender, a tragic historical farewell

**Values**:
- Loyalty and integrity
- It’s better to be broken into pieces than to be broken into pieces.
- Dignified choice in the face of desperate situations

**Historical background**:
- Time: The sixth day of February in the second year of Xiangxing in the Southern Song Dynasty (1279)
- Location: Yashan (now Yamen Town, Xinhui District, Jiangmen City, Guangdong Province)
-Characters: Lu Xiufu (Prime Minister Zuo), Zhang Shijie (Deputy Privy Envoy), Zhao Bing (Last Emperor of Song Dynasty, 7 years old)
- Significance: Marking the official end of the Southern Song Dynasty, the most tragic dynasty change in Chinese history

---

##Art Processing Principles

1. **Downplay the blood and gore**: Focus on expressing the sense of tragedy and the weight of history, and avoid over-exaggerating bloody scenes
2. **Emotional restraint**: Use blank space and hints to replace outright crying, and stop with silence.
3. **Impression creation**: extensive use of symbolic techniques (such as the setting sun, waves, lone boat)
4. **Music coordination**: Use traditional musical instruments such as guqin and chimes to create a sad atmosphere
