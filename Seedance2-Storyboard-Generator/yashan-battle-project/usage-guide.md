## Production Workflow

### Step 1: Material generation

Use Nana Banana Pro or other AI image generation tools to generate the required materials according to the prompt words in the material list.

**Priority Order**：
1. **Required** (core material):
- C01-C07: Various states of Lu Xiufu and Young Emperor
- C08-C09: Zhang Shijie’s two states
- C10-C11: Two states of Song Army soldiers
- S01-S10: all scene Assets
- P01-P04: core props (warship, jade seal)

2. **Optional** (as needed):
- C12-C17: other characters and group portraits
- P05-P11: other props

**Generation Notes**：
- Ensure uniform style: all materials use epic ink style prefixes
- Keep the characters consistent: The images of Lu Xiufu and Young Emperor should be consistent in each material
- Historical research: Costumes, props and symbols Resolution History of the late Southern Song Dynasty
- Emotional tone: maintain a tragic and solemn atmosphere

### Step 2: Video generation

Videos are generated by episodes using the Seedance 2.0 platform.

**E01Generation process**:
1. Upload S01 to the material location
2. Copy Seedance Prompt in E01 storyboard
3. Set the duration to 15 seconds
4. Generate video

**E02-E10 generation process**:
1. Upload @video_file_1 (previous episode video) and corresponding picture materials
2. Copy the Seedance Prompt of the corresponding episode number (note: E02-E10 all start with "Extend @video_file_1 by 15s")
3. Set the duration to 15 seconds
4. Generate video

**Important Notes**：
- E02-E10 must use the "Video Extension" feature to maintain continuity
-Upload up to 9 pictures per episode
- Make sure the material ID corresponds to the Upload Slot in the Storyboard Script

### Step 3: Video editing (optional)

Seedance 2.0 has limited video editing functions, and most modifications require regeneration. To edit:
1. Download the generated 10 episodes of video
2. Use external editing software for splicing
3. Add opening, ending, subtitles, BackgroundMusic, etc.

## HistoryBackgroundDescription

**Yashan Naval Battle** is one of the most tragic naval battles in Chinese history. It took place on the sixth day of February in the second year of Xiangxing in the Southern Song Dynasty (1279).

**Key Historical Figures**:
- **Lu Xiufu** (1236-1279): Prime Minister Zuo of the Southern Song Dynasty, he carried the young emperor on his back and threw himself into the sea to die for his country in the Battle of Yashan.
- **Zhang Shijie** (?-1279): Deputy Privy Envoy of the Southern Song Dynasty, commander-in-chief of the Song Army at the Yashan Sea Battle, and died for his country after the defeat.
- **Zhao Bing** (1272-1279): The last emperor of the Song Dynasty, who came to the throne at the age of 7, died for his country with Lu Xiufu in the Battle of Yashan.

**Historical Significance**:
- Marks the official end of the Southern Song Dynasty
- One hundred thousand soldiers and civilians threw themselves into the sea and died for their country, demonstrating the national integrity of "I would rather be in pieces than in ruins"
- The most tragic dynasty change in Chinese history

## FAQ

**Q: How to deal with sensitive content on historical themes? **
A: This script has adopted artistic expression techniques, focusing on expressing the sense of tragedy and the weight of history, and avoiding excessive exaggeration of gore. All material generation prompt words follow this principle.

**Q: How to ensure the accuracy of historical costumes and props? **
A: The prompt words in the material list already contain historical research information. You can refer to historical materials such as "Song History·Yu Fu Zhi" when generating.

**Q: How to maintain a unified style across ten episodes of video? **
A: Make sure all materials use the same style prefix. When each episode is generated, check whether the last frame of the previous episode is connected with the opening of the current episode.

**Q: Can the story length be shortened or extended? **
A: The total duration can be adjusted by adjusting the number of episodes or the duration of each episode. It is recommended to maintain a rhythm of 15 seconds/episode to keep the narrative flowing.

## Technical Reference

**Style Keywords**:
```
Epic Chinese traditional ink wash painting style with heavy historical atmosphere and tragic tone, similar to "Night Revels of Han Xizai" color system and "A Thousand Li of Rivers and Mountains" composition, Song Dynasty period, cinematic grandeur
```

**Camera Movement Vocabulary**:
- Zoom in - Enter scene
- Zoom out - Show the whole scene
- Pan - scan the battlefield
- Truck - follow the movement of the character
- Follow the camera (Follow) - Follow the figure who threw himself into the sea
- Crane - overlooking the battlefield
- Slow motion - emphasize key moments

**Music Style Suggestions**:
- Opening: low and pathos guqin solo
- Confrontation: intense war drums and string music
- Battle: fierce and tragic orchestral music
- Farewell: sad and sad Guqin music
- Throwing into the sea: orchestral music with extreme pathos
- Ending: The tragic gradually turns to calm, and finally returns to silence.

##Art Processing Principles

1. **Emotional restraint**: Use blank space and hints to replace straightforward crying, and use silence to stop
2. **Artistic conception creation**: extensive use of symbolic techniques (setting sun, waves, lone boat)
3. **Downplay the blood**: Focus on expressing the sense of tragedy and the weight of history
4. **Music coordination**: Use traditional musical instruments such as guqin, flute, and chimes to create a sad atmosphere
