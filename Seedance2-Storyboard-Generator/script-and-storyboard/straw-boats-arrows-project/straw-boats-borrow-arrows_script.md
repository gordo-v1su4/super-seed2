# Straw Boats Borrow Arrows - Script
---

## I. Core Hook
Crush with wisdom

---

## II. Story Synopsis

**Story Background**: At the end of the Eastern Han Dynasty, on the eve of the Battle of Chibi, Sun and Liu allied themselves against Cao Cao. Zhou Yu, the governor of Eastern Wu, was jealous of Zhuge Liang's talent and wanted to take the opportunity to get rid of him.

**Opening Conflict**: Zhou Yu used the excuse of lack of arrows in the army to make things difficult for Zhuge Liang to make 100,000 arrows within a three-day deadline, but in fact he set up a deadlock.

**Protagonist portrait**: Zhuge Liang (Mr. Wolong), Liu Bei's military advisor, feather fanscholar's headscarf, calm and composed, divine stratagem, knows astronomy from above and geography from below.

**Main event**: Zhou Yu made things difficult for him to make a military pledge → Zhuge Liang seemed unhurried → Nothing happened in the first two days → Heavy fog borrowed arrows in the early morning of the third day → The straw boat returned with a load of 100,000 arrows → Zhou Yu sighed to himself.

**Ending**: Zhuge Liang outsmarted one hundred thousand arrows, but Zhou Yu's plan failed, and he sighed to himself, "How can there be a bright future when there is a good one".

---

## III. One-Line Pitch
Making 100,000 arrows in three days? Zhou Yu wanted to get rid of Zhuge Liang, but he didn't know that Kong Ming had already determined the timing! On the heavy fog river, Straw Boats Borrow Arrows, crushing with wisdom, full of excitement!

## IV. Character Profiles

### Zhuge Liang
**Visual Image**: About 30 years old, with a face like a crown jewel, three long beards, wearing a scholar's headscarf, a crane-feather cloak, holding a feather fan, immortal elegance

**Identity Background**: Liu Bei's military advisor, nicknamed "Mr. Wolong", was invited by Liu Bei to settle in Eastern Wu to discuss the plan to resist Cao Cao

**Core Tags**: divine stratagem, calm and composed, strategizing, never misses a trick

**Personality**: Gentle and gentle on the outside, but insightful on the inside. No matter what kind of difficulties he faces, he always smiles and is confident. He is good at taking advantage of the right time and place to turn crisis points into opportunities.

**Signature Line**: "How can a general command the three armies if he doesn't know the weather, the right geography, or the people?"

---

### Zhou Yu
**Visual Image**: He is in his early 30s, handsome and handsome, with a heroic spirit between his brows, wearing Eastern Wu general armor and a sword on his waist.

**Identity Background**: Eastern Wu Governor, commander of the Wu army, extremely intelligent but narrow-minded, jealous of Zhuge Liang's talent

**Core Tags**: narrow-minded, jealous of talent, extremely talented, self-destructive

**Personality**: Enthusiastic and bold on the surface, but jealous on the inside. No one can tolerate the existence of people smarter than themselves. He always wanted to make things difficult for Zhuge Liang, but he shot himself in the foot every time.

**Signature Line**: "Zhuge Liang! I'll see how you die this time!"

---

### Lu Su
**Visual Image**: About 40 years old, honest-looking, honest and loyal, wearing civilian clothes

**Identity Background**: Eastern Wu counselor, Zhou Yu's subordinate, upright and kind-hearted, is the messenger between Zhuge Liang and Zhou Yu

**Core Tags**: honest and loyal, kind, worried about others

**Personality**: Loyal and kind-hearted, easily taken advantage of by Zhou Yu but sympathetic to Zhuge Liang. Torn between Zhou Yu and Zhuge Liang.

**Signature Line**: "Sir, can these one hundred thousand arrows... really be made?"

---

### Cao Cao
**Visual Image**: About 50 years old, majestic and domineering, wearing a dragon-patterned jersey, with sharp eyes and no anger.

**Identity Background**: King Wei, commanding millions of troops, stationed in Jiangbei, confronting Eastern Wu across the river

**Core Tags**: suspicious, cautious, domineering

**Personality**: Suspicious by nature, cautious in using troops. Facing an unknown situation, I would rather kill a thousand by mistake than let one go.

**Signature Line**: "Heavy fog and misty river, there must be an ambush! Order the crossbowmen to release arrows!"

## V. Script Outline

### Act I (Setup)
Zhou Yu used the excuse that Eastern Wu was short of arrows and made it difficult for Zhuge Liang to make 100,000 arrows within a three-day deadline, which was actually setting up a dead end. Zhuge Liang calmly agreed and made a military pledge. Zhou Yu was secretly happy, thinking that he could finally get rid of Zhuge Liang this time.

### Act II (Rising)（Rising Action/Turning Point）
On the first and second days, Zhuge Liang made no movement at all, just drinking and having fun. Lu Su was extremely anxious and urged her many times, but Zhuge Liang had a plan in mind. Zhou Yu sent people to monitor him, thinking that Zhuge Liang had nothing to do. In the early morning of the third day, Zhuge Liang invited Lu Su to go to the river with him, and Straw Boats Borrow Arrows started.

### Act III (Climax)(Climax)
Heavy fog filled the Yangtze River, and Zhuge Liang led twenty straw boats approaching Cao camp. The drums were beaten and Cao Jun was shocked. Cao Cao was suspicious by nature and did not dare to send troops, so he ordered thousands of arrows to be fired. Arrows rained down, and all of them hit the scarecrow on the straw boat. The straw boat received arrows from both sides and returned home with a full load.

### Resolution
Zhuge Liang returned with a load of 100,000 arrows, and Zhou Yu was stunned. Lu Su told the borrowed arrow that he had passed by, and Zhou Yu sighed: "Since Yu is born, how can Liang be born!"
