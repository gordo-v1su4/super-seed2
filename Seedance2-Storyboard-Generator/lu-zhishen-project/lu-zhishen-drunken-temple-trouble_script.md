#Lu Zhishen gets into trouble in the temple after being drunk - script

## 1. Core Hook
Rough man making trouble with Buddha

## 2. Story Synopsis

**Story background**: During the Northern Song Dynasty, Wenshu Monastery in Mount Wutai. Lu Zhishen became a monk to avoid disaster after defeating Guanxi with his fists, and his Buddhist name was "Zhishen".

**Opening Conflict**: Lu Zhishen couldn't stand the Buddhist rules and regulations, secretly went down the mountain to drink, and returned to the temple drunk.

**Portrait of the Protagonist**: Lu Zhishen, a burly man with a height of eight feet and a waist of ten girth. He has a bold personality, hates evil, has a bad temper, and lacks patience.

**Main plot event**: Lu Zhishen returned to Wenshu Monastery after being drunk → was blocked by the gatekeeper monk → beat up the Vajra statue angrily → made a scene in the Zen hall → was sent to the Daxiangguo Temple in Tokyo by Elder Zhizhen.

**Ending**: Lu Zhishen was sent to Tokyo to start the next stage of his career.

## 3. One-Line Pitch
Even a Buddha can't stop a drunkard and a coward! The flower monk Lu Zhishen was drunk and attacked Mount Wutai, and Vajra Bodhisattva had to give way!

## 4. Character Profiles

**Lu Zhishen**
- Visual image: About 30 years old, eight feet tall, with a face as thick as a jujube, a beard, a ring scar on his bald head, a tall and muscular body, wearing a gray monk's robe but with a murderous look
- Identity background: Originally a Tiha, he became a monk because of killing people to avoid disaster, and his dharma name is Zhishen.
- Core tags: jealous of evil, violent and heroic, wine and meat to the guts, powerful and none poor
-Characteristics: straightforward and righteous, but lacks self-control, cannot stand Buddhist rules, and gets into trouble whenever he drinks
- Golden sentence: "The Sa family just can't control this mouth in this life!"

**Elder Zhizhen**
-Visual image: About 60 years old, with white eyebrows and long beard, a kind and naughty face, wearing a yellow cassock and holding a Zen staff
- Identity background: Abbot of Wenshu Monastery in Mount Wutai
- Core tags: high moral character, compassion, and insight into people's hearts
-Character traits: Seeing that Lu Zhishen’s fate is not over, he is both tolerant and helpless.
- Golden sentence: "It is difficult for Buddhism to retain this child's heart before his fate is over."

**Gatekeeper Monk**
-Visual image: About 20 years old, thin and withered, with a frightened face, wearing a gray monk's robe
- Identity background: Gatekeeper of Wenshu Monastery
- Core tags: timid and conscientious
-Character traits: Honest and frightened by Lu Zhishen

## 5. Script Outline

**Act I (Setup)**: Lu Zhishen drank heavily in the tavern at the foot of the mountain and returned to Wenshu Monastery drunkenly

**Act II (Rising Action)**: The gatekeeper monk blocked it, Lu Zhishen was angry at Setup, and broke the Vajra statue at the mountain gate.

**Act III (Climax) (Turning Point/Climax)**: Lu Zhishen made a big fuss in the meditation hall, the monks were scared away, and the scene was chaotic.

**Epilogue (End)**: Elder Zhizhen appeared, and after sighing, he decided to send Lu Zhishen to Daxiangguo Temple in Tokyo.

### Episode 2 Fighting King Kong in Fury

**2-1 [Sunday] [Outside] Wenshu Monastery Mountain Gate**
Props: Mountain gate, King Kong statue
Characters: Lu Zhishen, gatekeeper monk

△ [Empty Mirror] The gate of Wenshu Monastery is majestic, with two Vajra statues standing majestically with their eyes wide open.
△ Medium shot, the gatekeeper monk is blocking the mountain gate with both hands and looking down the mountain nervously.
△ Following the camera, Lu Zhishen appeared from the Turning Point corner of the mountain road, knocking down the stone scripture building on the roadside.

△ Close-up, Lu Zhishen stopped, squinted his eyes, and approached the gatekeeper with his face.

△ Lu Zhishen Turning Point punched King Kong to the right again, and King Kong's arm was broken and collapsed.

### Episode 3: Big Trouble in the Zen Hall

**3-1 [Sunday] [Inside] Wenshu Monastery Zen Hall**
Props: futon, Zen seat, Buddha statue
Characters: Lu Zhishen, monks

△ [Empty Mirror] In the Zen hall, monks are meditating and chanting sutras, cigarette smoke is lingering, and Sanskrit sounds are heard.
△ Medium shot, Lu Zhishen kicks open the door of the meditation hall. The wooden door hits the wall with a bang, and dust falls.
△ The monks raised their heads in panic, the chanting stopped suddenly, and they all looked towards the door.
△ Follow the camera, Lu Zhishen strides in, the monk's robe is open and his chest is exposed, and he smells of alcohol.
△ Lu Zhishen walked to the nearest monk and pushed his futon away
Lu Zhishen: You bald donkeys, why are you pretending to be serious?
△ Close-up, the old monk’s hands are shaking and the scriptures fall to the ground
△ Lu Zhishen looked around and saw the Buddha statue in the hall, his eyes became even more angry.
△ Lu Zhishen rushed towards the Buddha statue and kicked him. The Buddha statue shook, the incense burner overturned, and the charcoal fire rolled to the ground.
△ The monks were frightened and fled in all directions. The meditation hall was in chaos, scriptures were flying around, and futons were trampled and staggered.
△ Lu Zhishen stood in the middle of the Zen hall, waving his arms with a frantic look on his face.
Lu Zhishen (roaring): Sajia wants wine! Sajia wants meat! This ruined temple is untenable!
[Subtitle: Chaos in the meditation hall, everyone flees]
